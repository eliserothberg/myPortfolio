In the beginning...

Ask lots of questions. And then a few more. What is the purpose and desired outcome? What is the scope? What are the "must haves", the "like to haves", and the "absolutely can't haves"? Who is our user- what do they like, what are they all about? Are we starting from scratch, or is there a previous site/design/blueprint that needs to be followed?
Is 42 really the answer?

Measure Twice, cut once.

What, if anything, is already out there, and what can we learn from it?  What worked; what didn’t? Which packages, frameworks, and/or databases should be used- what's best suited for the tasks at hand? What is the most efficient way to deliver the best possible product in a timely manner?

Make it and break it.

Pseudo code it.
Build it.
Break it.
Rebuild it.
Rinse, repeat, refactor as needed.

Classic Cocktail Hangman
Players guess the names of cocktails with a piece of a martini glass appearing for every wrong guess. Key events listen for the letters that players type. Wins, losses, the number of guess left, letters guessed, and dashes that get replaced with letters are displayed. If the player chooses to play again, the game is reset and another word chosen and dashes displayed.

State Laws Trivia
Multiple choice trivia game that shows one question until it's answered or time runs out. When the correct answer is selected, a congratulations screen with a relevant gif appears. If time runs out or a wrong answer is selected, the correct answer and a gif appears. Without user input, the next question is displayed after a few seconds.
The final screen shows the number of correct answers, incorrect answers, and an option to restart the game (without reloading the page).
All answer options are illegal in at least one American state.
